  • project objective

    Create a Google Doodle style CSS animation that celebrates the 40th anniversary of David Bowie’s album most underrated album: Lodger, released May 18th, 1979.

    target audience

    Google users, David Bowie enthusiasts.

    audience needs

    Something fun and unobtrusive so they can still use the search engine.

    deliverables

    Animated Doodle web paged based on Google’s home page.

  • insights

    As a big fan of David Bowie, I enjoy celebrating the anniversaries of his classic albums. In 2017, when I made this project, it was too late to do anything for the anniversaries of his very celebrated 1977 releases: Low and “Heroes.” There was a gap in his releases in 1978 while he was on his world tour: Isolar II. 1979 was his next big releases, although Lodger was not a commercial or critical success at the time it has been recognized in hindsight as one of his most influential albums, being an often-cited influence for 90s Britpop bands. The animation I created for this album’s anniversary is based on Lodger’s strange original cover, I essentially recreated the cover but with more Google related text on the post card. I replaced the address of RCA, his record label at the time, with the address of Google’s London office and changed the album title to “Google” and used a persistent animation to keep the letters pulsing after the original animation was complete.
