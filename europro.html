europro website
  • project objective

    Update EuroPro's website to match their more refined visual identity.

    target audience

    European car enthusiasts and those who are simply looking for a repair shop in Spokane. EuroPro also sells vehicles, however this took a backseat on this project.

    build method

    Wordpress

  • audience needs

    This website is a relatively one primarily intended to present business information, meaning the most important goal was to ensure that it is accessible on all kinds of devices.

    deliverables

    A responsive website that utilizes up-to-date photography and content.

project insights
my role: web designer

The old EuroPro website contained much of the relevant information that a visitor to the site might need, but the presentation was confusing and unpolished. My main goal with this project was to refine the visual aesthetic of the website with updated icons, photography, and content organization. This meant building the website with a more modern and clean aesthetic in mind. To start this process, I stripped all of the necessary images and text off of the old site and organized it by relevance. I then began working on conceptualizing a site map that the content was organized into.

After some rounds of sketching, I began aggregating all of the new site content. This included the bios on the about page. With everything collected, I started building the site in line with EuroPro's updated identity standards guide. The project's scope was small and was intended to bring their website on par with the quality of their social media presence.
